## What to research

1. **Israeli Central Bureau of Statistics (הלמ"ס / CBS)** — does it publish PTSD prevalence data? Search at www.cbs.gov.il. Look for:
   - National health surveys (specifically "סקר הבריאות הלאומי" / National Health Survey)
   - Mental health statistics publications
   - Social survey (הסקר החברתי) results related to mental health

2. **Israeli Ministry of Health (משרד הבריאות)** — mental health data:
   - Search for PTSD prevalence figures
   - Any mental health epidemiology reports
   - Statistics on PTSD specifically vs. anxiety disorders broadly

3. **Israeli academic/clinical sources**:
   - Published studies on PTSD prevalence in Israel (Israel Journal of Psychiatry, etc.)
   - The Natal organization (נטל — Israel Trauma Center for Victims of Terror and War) — they publish statistics on combat PTSD
   - Beit Natan, ERAN, or other Israeli trauma organizations

4. **Context-specific research**:
   - Combat PTSD specifically (IDF veterans) — this population is large in Israel
   - Civilian PTSD (terror attacks, accidents, violence survivors)
   - Holocaust survivors and second-generation PTSD — often cited in Israeli mental health literature
   - General population prevalence rate (%) vs. absolute numbers

5. **If no exact Israeli figure is findable**, document:
   - The best available Israeli figure (even if dated or estimated)
   - The international prevalence rate (usually cited as 3–4% of general population, or up to 20% in high-trauma populations)
   - The Israeli population total (approximately 9.8 million as of 2024, per CBS)
   - A derived estimate with clear methodology: "Israel's population × international prevalence rate"
