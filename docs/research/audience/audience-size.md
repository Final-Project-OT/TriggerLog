### Authoritative figure (if found)

**Best citable figure for a student presentation:**

> **580,000 Israelis** suffer from at least one severe PTSD symptom as a direct consequence of October 7, 2023 and its aftermath.

- **Source:** State Comptroller of Israel (מבקר המדינה), Matanyahu Englman
- **Report date:** February 12, 2025
- **Report type:** Official government audit report on mental health following October 7
- **URL (press coverage):** https://www.timesofisrael.com/state-comptroller-says-3-million-israelis-suffering-from-trauma-since-october-7/
  and https://www.jpost.com/health-and-wellness/mind-and-spirit/article-841646

**Additional figure from the same report (broader scope):**

> **~3 million Israelis** (≈38% of the adult population) experienced symptoms of anxiety, depression, or PTSD following October 7.

This broader number conflates PTSD with depression and anxiety and is less precise for a PTSD-specific slide. The 580,000 figure isolates "at least one severe PTSD symptom."

---

**Supporting academic figure (for corroboration):**

> **~520,000 Israelis** (5.3% of total population; range 160,000–880,000) are projected to develop PTSD as a result of October 7 and the war.

- **Source:** Katsoty D., Greidinger M., Neria Y., Segev A., Lurie I. — peer-reviewed prediction model
- **Journal:** *Israel Journal of Health Policy Research* (peer-reviewed, published by BMC/Springer)
- **Publication date:** October 29, 2024
- **DOI:** 10.1186/s13584-024-00644-6
- **PMC URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11520871/
- **Affiliated institutions:** Hebrew University, Shaare Zedek Hospital, Columbia University, Effective Altruism Israel

---

**Pre-October 7 baseline (peer-reviewed population study):**

> **11.6% of the Israeli general population** was living with PTSD or complex PTSD (cPTSD) as of 2018 — representing approximately **950,000–1,000,000 people** (applied to the ~8.6 million population at that time).
> Broken down: PTSD alone = 9.0%; cPTSD = 2.6%.

- **Source:** Ben-Ezra M. et al., "Posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and complex PTSD (CPTSD) as per ICD-11 proposals: A population study in Israel"
- **Journal:** *Depression and Anxiety*, Vol. 35(3), pp. 264–274
- **Year:** 2018
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29451956/
- **Sample:** n=1,003 (nationally representative)
- **Note:** This is a symptom-based prevalence study using the ICD-11 ITQ instrument, not administrative diagnosis counts. The high rate reflects Israel's unusually high cumulative trauma exposure (military service, terror attacks, security situation).

---

**IDF/Defense Ministry data (combat veterans specifically):**

> As of 2025, Israel's Defense Ministry Rehabilitation Department cares for ~82,400 wounded veterans total; approximately **31,000 (38%) suffer from mental health conditions** including PTSD. Since October 7, **22,000 soldiers** were treated — 58% for PTSD or mental health conditions. By 2028, Israel expects **100,000 IDF veterans** to be officially recognized as wounded, half with severe PTSD.

- **Source:** Israeli Ministry of Defense Rehabilitation Department (אגף השיקום, משרד הביטחון)
- **Coverage:** https://www.timesofisrael.com/over-half-of-soldiers-treated-in-rehab-centers-have-mental-health-issues-stats-show/
- **Note:** Only formally recognized/disabled veterans are counted here; many more soldiers have PTSD without formal ministry recognition.

### Fallback if no Israeli figure is found

*(This section is kept for reference — Israeli figures were found, so the fallback is not needed for the presentation.)*

Had no Israeli-specific figure been available, the derived estimate would be:

- Israeli population (2024): ~9.8 million (CBS/הלמ"ס)
- International PTSD prevalence rate: 3.9% (WHO/global average) — but Israel's rate is documented as substantially higher
- Conservative derived estimate: 9,800,000 × 3.9% = **~382,000**
- Israel-adjusted estimate (using Ben-Ezra 9% PTSD-only rate): 9,800,000 × 9% = **~882,000**

The actual figures from official Israeli sources (580,000 severe-symptom PTSD post-Oct-7; ~950,000 applying the 2018 11.6% baseline) fall within this range, validating the derived approach.

#### Key caveats for presenters

1. **The 580,000 figure is symptom-based, not diagnosis-based.** The State Comptroller's report derives from a population poll (April 2024) where 38% of adults reported at least one symptom; 16% reported severe symptoms. The 580,000 represents the "severe symptoms" subset. It is the most conservative and defensible for citing as PTSD specifically.

2. **The 3 million figure includes depression and anxiety**, not PTSD alone. Do not cite this as a PTSD-only number.

3. **No CBS/הלמ"ס standalone PTSD time-series exists.** CBS publishes general mental disorder data but PTSD is not broken out in their regular statistical releases. The best official Israeli source for PTSD specifically is the State Comptroller's February 2025 report.

4. **The pre-war Israeli PTSD rate was already unusually high** — Ben-Ezra et al.'s 9% PTSD prevalence vs. the 3.9% global average reflects decades of cumulative conflict and security trauma exposure. Israel is not comparable to other OECD countries on this metric.

5. **The original placeholder of ~280,000** is plausible as a pre-war figure for formally diagnosed (treated) PTSD patients but no source has been located confirming exactly this number. It likely derives from applying the international 3% rate to Israel's ~9.4M population circa 2020. The actual diagnosed/treated number would be lower than the prevalence number due to Israel's systemic mental health access barriers (90% of those in need do not receive treatment, per the State Comptroller).

**Verbally say:** "לפי דוח מבקר המדינה מפברואר 2025, כ-580,000 ישראלים סובלים מתסמיני PTSD חמורים כתוצאה ישירה מהמלחמה — ואנחנו מדברים על קהל שגדל מדי יום."
